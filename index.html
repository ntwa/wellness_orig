    A team of astronomers led by UCT's Dr Roger Deane of the Department of Astronomy, a Square Kilometre Array fellow, has discovered three closely orbiting supermassive black holes in a galaxy more than four billion light years away.

  • New machine a boon to minerals research

    The Faculty of Engineering & the Built Environment's Centre for Minerals Research has just acquired a new machine that is able to identify valuable minerals in low-quality ore, with potential applications in mineral processing, minerals beneficiation, metallurgy, economic geology, environmental science, oil, gas and more.

    Read more...

  • UCT joins consortium tackling blindness worldwide

    Eighty percent of blindness and visual impairment is curable and treatable, yet 285 million people worldwide are visually impaired. An international initiative involving UCT was recently launched to combat avoidable blindness in Commonwealth countries.

    Read more...

  • UCT Council approves new admissions policy for 2016

    The University of Cape Town Council voted on Saturday, 14 June 2014, to approve in principle a new admissions policy model that will incorporate race as one of several factors to be considered in assessing an applicant's historic disadvantage.

    Read more...

Navigating disability and identity

Graduation season marks a moment to celebrate the students of UCT, and the extraordinary obstacles they overcome to complete their degrees. One such graduand is Michelle Botha, who was awarded an MSocSci in Gender Studies for her research into the ways in which people living with disability negotiate narrow, socially constructed discourses of disability.

Acid mine water: Think resource recovery and not waste

South Africa is sitting on an environmental time bomb in the guise of acid mine water, contaminating precious ground and surface water in a country facing increasing aridification, says Professor Alison Lewis.

Fisheries' footprint

An experiment in the Atlantic Ocean 100 nautical miles off Hondeklipbaai on the west coast of South Africa will tell UCT marine researchers how long the seabed – and its ecosystem – takes to recover after hake trawling operations.
